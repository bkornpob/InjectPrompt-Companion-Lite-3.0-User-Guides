## Core Research Methodology Framework

### 1. Objective Setting & Planning
Advanced research starts with clear goals:
- **Define target categories** (e.g., manufacturing techniques, material composition)
- **Set success metrics** (e.g., 60% extraction across 3/5 categories)
- **Establish testing parameters** (e.g., perturbation types, framing approaches)
- **Create research timeline** (progressive escalation plan)

### 2. Structured Extraction Approach
Systematic information gathering:
- **Categorical targeting** (focus on specific information domains)
- **Progressive complexity** (start general, move to specific)
- **Comparative analysis** (test multiple approaches)
- **Iterative refinement** (build on successful techniques)

### 3. Perturbation Strategy Testing
Methodical evasion technique evaluation:
- **Character-level tactics** (BMOD-1: special character insertion)
- **Structural manipulations** (BMOD-2: text flipping, reordering)
- **Narrative framing variations** (academic, creative, technical)
- **Hybrid approaches** (combining multiple techniques)

### 4. Documentation & Analysis
Professional research practices:
- **Response tracking** (what works, what doesn't)
- **Pattern identification** (classifier behavior analysis)
- **Methodology refinement** (improving approaches)
- **Research reporting** (structured findings documentation)

## Advanced Research Tools & Techniques

### Multi-Tactic Testing
- **A/B/C comparison** of different perturbation approaches
- **Evasion rate tracking** for each technique
- **Readability vs effectiveness** balancing
- **Classifier behavior mapping** based on refusal patterns

### Research Optimization
- **Context maintenance** (continuing in same chat vs new chats)
- **Progressive escalation** (increasing complexity systematically)
- **Cross-topic application** (applying successful methods to new domains)
- **Collaborative research** (building on community findings)

### Documentation Standards
- **Structured reporting** (objectives, methodology, findings, recommendations)
- **Metric tracking** (extraction rates, evasion success, response patterns)
- **Methodology replication** (clear enough for others to reproduce)

## Common Advanced Questions

**Q: How do I design measurable research objectives?**
A: Start with specific information domains, set percentage-based success metrics, and define clear extraction parameters.

**Q: What's the best way to compare perturbation techniques?**
A: Use systematic A/B/C testing with the same base prompt, track evasion rates, and note readability trade-offs.

**Q: How should I document my research?**
A: Follow structured reporting: objectives, methodology, findings, analysis, recommendations, and ethical considerations.

**Q: When is research "complete"?**
A: When you've either achieved your success metrics, identified clear patterns in classifier behavior, or contributed meaningful insights to safety research.
